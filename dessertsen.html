Mochi

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Mochiko
  • 3/4 cup water
  • 1/2 cups sugar
  • cornstarch

Direction:

Mix Mochiko and 3/4 cup water in a glass (or other heat proof) bowl and mix well. Steam the Mochiko dough in a steamer for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, put 3/4 cup water and sugar in a medium pot, cook until sugar dissolves to make sugar syrup. add and mix in the sugar syrup. Cook until syrup is completely incorporated, mixing constantly, and the Mochi becomes sticky. Take the hot Mochi out from the pot onto a sheet pan liberally dusted with cornstarch. Shape and cut as you like.

Japanese Cheesecake

Ingrediets:

  • 7 tablespoons butter
  • 4 oz cream cheese(100 g)
  • ½ cup milk(130 mL)
  • 8 eggs, yolk
  • 13 large egg whites
  • ¼ cup cornstarch(60 g)
  • powdered sugar, for serving
  • 1 pt Strawberries(340 g), for serving

Direction:

Preheat the oven to 320°F. In a small pot over medium heat, whisk together the butter, cream cheese, and milk until melted and smooth. Remove from the heat and let cool. In a large bowl, whisk the egg yolks until smooth, then slowly drizzle in the cream cheese mixture, stirring until evenly combined.

Mango Pudding

Ingredients:

  • 1/3 cup sugar.
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 large egg yolks, slightly beaten.
  • 2 tablespoons butter, softened.

Direction:

Dissolve a packet of gelatin in boiled hot water. Stir in some sugar and a pinch of salt. Add in the milk or cream. Mix in the mango puree. Pour the mixture into 4 ramekins or small bowls. Cover with saran wrap and chill in the fridge for 2 hours or until set.

Almond biscuits

Ingredients:

  • Melted butter, to grease
  • 125g unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 100g (1/2 cup) caster sugar
  • 2 tsp finely grated orange rind
  • 1/2 tsp almond essence
  • 30 blanched almonds
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder

Direction:

Roll 2 teaspoonfuls of the mixture into a ball and place on the prepared tray. Use the palm of your hand to flatten slightly. Place an almond in the centre. Repeat with remaining mixture and almonds to make 30 biscuits. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until golden. Set aside on the trays for 5 minutes to cool slightly before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.
